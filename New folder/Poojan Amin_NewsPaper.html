Ahmedabad: All that glitters is indeed gold, at least for Indians this year! India's gold imports in FY25 shot up by a stunning 38% to a decade-high of 855 tonnes, despite record-high prices. According to data from Ahmedabad Air Cargo Complex, the import volume that was highest in 10 years. "Lower import >duty has helped to improve the demand.

Moreover, there is strong demand from the jewellery industry in Gujarat and Mumbai," said an official at the complex. The average gold price in FY25 was ₹60,400/10 gm—highest in a financial year ever—up from ₹51,800/10 gm in FY24.

However, the import duty cut from 12.5% to 10% helped offset the cost. "Imports remained strong due to weddings and festivals. Also, investors bought gold as a safe haven amid geopolitical uncertainties and global market volatility," said Mukesh Kothari, director, RiddiSiddhi Bullions Ltd (RSBL).

Mumbai: There was bloodbath across global stock markets as US President Donald Trump's move to impose harsh tariffs on Chinese goods triggered a full-blown trade war and global recession fears. After Friday's market selloff following Trump's statement, the Indian markets opened sharply lower on Monday with the Sensex and Nifty falling nearly 4%.

The BSE Sensex crashed 2,227 points to 72,739 while the NSE Nifty fell 691 points to 21,945. The rupee weakened to 83.50 against the US dollar as investors moved to safe havens like gold and bonds. "Markets are reacting to global uncertainty.

Investors are dumping equities," said Aishwarya Kumar of Kotak Securities. The day's sell off left investors poorer by Rs 14.2 lakh crore with BSE's market capitalisation now at Rs 389.3 lakh crore. This was the third biggest single day plunge in investors wealth ever.
Among the 30 Sensex constituents, only one, HUL, closed with a marginal gain. Top fund managers and strategists said world markets were in unknown territory, and hence risks are high. However, they said given India's small share in global trade, the impact could be limited.
New Delhi: The Centre has raised LPG and excise duty on petrol and diesel, but officials clarified that this will not raise prices for consumers due to softening crude oil rates and recent fuel price cuts. Domestic LPG price was hiked by ₹25 per cylinder. At the same time, excise duty on petrol was increased by ₹2/litre and diesel by ₹1/litre.
The new duty structure will generate ₹37K crore in additional revenue annually for the government. "With this move, we are correcting duty imbalances and shoring up revenues," said a finance ministry official.

Oil minister Hardeep Singh Puri defended both the moves, saying they were required in view of rising benchmark rates leaving a Rs 41,338 crore hole in the oil market companies' pockets in 2024-25 despite govt subsidy for 10.3 crore Ujjwala beneficiaries
